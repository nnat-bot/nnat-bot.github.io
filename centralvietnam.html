Da Nang: A Coastal Gem in Central Vietnam

Located along the South China Sea in central Vietnam, Da Nang has emerged as a top travel destination for international visitors. With its wide range of attractions and activities, the city offers countless opportunities to create unforgettable memories. From breathtaking natural scenery to rich cultural experiences, Da Nang promises a journey full of excitement and discovery. This guide provides essential information to help you explore everything the city has to offer.

Geographically, Da Nang lies between Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City—764 kilometers south of Hanoi and 964 kilometers north of Ho Chi Minh City. It borders Thua Thien Hue Province to the north, with Hue City just 108 kilometers away. To the west and south, it shares boundaries with Quang Nam Province, while the eastern side faces the vast South China Sea.

Dragon Bridge: Shaped like a dragon, this iconic structure breathes fire every weekend at 9 PM. Spectators can enjoy the show from the bridge itself or nearby viewpoints along the Han River.

Marble Mountains One of Da Nang's most famous landmarks, the Marble Mountains consist of six limestone peaks: Kim Son, Moc Son, Thuy Son, Tho Son, Duong Hoa Son, and Am Hoa Son. Covering nearly 2 square kilometers, this site holds both natural beauty and cultural significance. Recognized as a national relic in 1980 and upgraded to a special national site in 2019, it hosts the annual Quan The Am Festival, which celebrates Vietnamese spirituality and tradition.

Son Tra Peninsula Situated in Son Tra District, this peninsula rises 693 meters above sea level and spans 60 square kilometers. It is home to a lush forest and rare wildlife, including the red-shanked douc langur, often referred to as the “Queen of Primates.” The area also features diverse flora and fauna, making it a must-visit for nature lovers.

My Khe Beach located in Phuoc My Ward, My Khe Beach stretches for about 900 meters and is one of the most popular beaches in Da Nang. It was ranked among Asia's top 10 beaches by Tripadvisor. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing at nearby resorts and restaurants.

Hue: Vietnam' Historic Capital

Hue is widely recognized as Vietnam's former imperial capital, a title it held from 1801 until 1945. Although it may not be as ancient as some might assume, Hue played a crucial role in shaping the country's cultural identity during the reign of the Nguyen dynasty. Interestingly, this dynasty is also responsible for the widespread use of the surname “Nguyen,” which is now shared by approximately 39% of the Vietnamese population.

Explore the Imperial City: The Imperial City, also known as the Hue Citadel and Forbidden City, stands as the most iconic historical site in Hue. Covering over 5 square kilometers, it features a vast array of architectural and cultural landmarks, including:

  • The Meridian Gate
  • Dai Noi (the traditional name for the Citadel)
  • Phung Tien Palace
  • Dien Tho Palace
  • Truong Sanh Palace
  • Kien Trung Palace

This complex offers a deep insight into Vietnam's imperial past and is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Stroll Along the “Golden Mile” Situated just south of the Perfume River, the “Golden Mile” refers to the stretch of Le Loi Street between Ha Noi Street and Chu Van An. It is considered one of the most pleasant areas for walking, especially for first-time visitors. The nearby streets—Doi Cung, Pham Ngu Lao, and Vo Thi Sau—are equally charming and are closed to traffic on weekend evenings. This allows pedestrians to enjoy traditional performances and street food in a relaxed, car-free environment. An evening walk along the Golden Mile offers a wonderful opportunity to experience the vibrant atmosphere of modern Hue.

Nha Trang: Vietnam's Sunlit Coastal Paradise

Nha Trang is a lively seaside town renowned for its abundant sunshine—boasting more sunny days annually than any other location in Vietnam. Its main attraction, Tran Phu Beach, stretches for seven kilometers along the coast, offering soft white sand, crystal-clear waters, swaying palm trees, and vibrant beachside bars and seafood eateries.

One of the city's most captivating features is its stunning bay, dotted with 19 islands and islets. Tran Phu Beach is a hub of activity, where visitors relax on sun loungers and enjoy a wide range of water sports, including parasailing, kite surfing, paddle boarding, and jet skiing. For a quieter experience, travelers can take a motorbike ride to Long Beach, located just outside the city. This peaceful spot features shallow turquoise waters and local restaurants serving freshly caught seafood. For a scenic day trip, head 60 kilometers north to Doc Let Beach, considered one of the most picturesque coastal areas in Vietnam.

No visit to Nha Trang is complete without experiencing its famous mud baths. The mineral-rich clay is believed to offer therapeutic benefits, such as improved circulation and skin hydration. After soaking in the warm, sticky mud, visitors can rinse off under cool waterfalls and unwind in hot mineral pools. For those seeking extra indulgence, treatments like coffee massages and chocolate body wraps are available. Among the three mud bath facilities in the city, I-Resort is the most popular. It also features a large water park with slides, pools, and exciting water activities—making it a perfect destination for families and groups.

Accommodation in Nha Trang is another highlight. The city offers a wide range of options, from luxurious beachfront resorts to peaceful retreats and centrally located hotels. The rugged coastline between Cam Ranh Airport and Nha Trang is currently undergoing development, with ten new resorts already in progress, promising even more choices for future visitors.

Phu Yen: Vietnam's Hidden Gem on the Central Coast

Phu Yen is a province located in central Vietnam, bordered by a dramatic mountain range to the west and over 200 kilometers of coastline to the east. Traditionally an agricultural region, Phu Yen has only recently begun to attract attention as a tourist destination. Its rise in popularity is largely credited to the 2015 Vietnamese film Toi Thay Hoa Vang Tren Co Xanh (Dear Brother), which showcased the province's natural beauty.

Tuy Hoa, the provincial capital, is situated roughly halfway between the well-known beach cities of Nha Trang and Quy Nhon. Its strategic location makes it an ideal stop for travelers journeying along Vietnam's central coast. Despite its accessibility, Tuy Hoa remains relatively untouched by mass tourism, offering a peaceful atmosphere. The city is thoughtfully designed, with wide, well-maintained streets forming a neat grid along the river. Tuy Hoa Beach runs along the eastern edge of the city, featuring a scenic promenade and a tranquil shoreline.

Bai Mon is a secluded beach nestled between rugged mountains. For a truly memorable experience, visitors are encouraged to arrive at dawn and hike up the winding path to Mui Dien Lighthouse. From this vantage point—Vietnam's second easternmost tip—the sunrise over the ocean is breathtaking. A second staircase leads down to Bai Mon Beach, which feels like a hidden paradise surrounded by cliffs and greenery.

While Bai Xep is perhaps the most well-known destination in Phu Yen, Da Dia Reef stands out as its most extraordinary natural wonder. Formed by volcanic activity, the reef features striking hexagonal basalt columns that resemble a giant honeycomb. These geometric formations cascade into the sea, creating a surreal landscape reminiscent of a video game. Nearby, a beach offers refreshments and a walking path that leads to another lighthouse, providing panoramic views of the ocean and the unique rock formations.

Quy Nhon: A Coastal Treasure in Central Vietnam

Nestled along Vietnam's central coastline, Quy Nhon enjoys a stunning natural setting, framed by pristine beaches and verdant mountains. Its location makes it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and cultural exploration. The climate in Quy Nhon is a blend of northern and southern Vietnam's weather patterns, characterized by a tropical, semi-humid atmosphere. From January to August, the city experiences hot and dry conditions, with temperatures often reaching the mid-30s Celsius, particularly from May onward. As autumn approaches, temperatures begin to cool. Rainfall increases during the winter months, peaking in October and November. Although typhoons occasionally bring strong winds and heavy rain, flooding is rarely reported.

Located on the Phuong Mai Peninsula, approximately 25 kilometers northeast of Quy Nhon, Ky Co Beach is a tropical paradise. Its powdery sands and turquoise waters create a serene environment perfect for swimming, snorkeling, or simply relaxing amidst the surrounding cliffs and greenery. Nearby, Eo Gio—meaning “Windy Strait”—offers dramatic coastal scenery and refreshing sea breezes. Towering cliffs plunge into the ocean, forming a breathtaking landscape that captivates visitors.

Situated in Nhon Chau Commune and accessible by boat, Cu Lao Xanh Island is a hidden gem known for its untouched beauty. Visitors are welcomed by emerald waters, quiet beaches, and lush vegetation. Activities such as snorkeling, coastal walks, and peaceful relaxation make this island an ideal escape from the city.

Located in Phuoc Hiep Commune, about 25 kilometers from Quy Nhon, the Banh It Towers are a remarkable example of Champa architecture. Surrounded by greenery, these ancient structures offer a glimpse into the region's historical and spiritual past. They are a must-see for those interested in Vietnam's cultural legacy.

Quang Tri: A Land of History and Natural Beauty

Located in Central Vietnam, Quang Tri is a province known for its resilience and historical significance. Often referred to as a “living museum,” it preserves the heroic legacy of Vietnam’s revolutionary past. Today, Quang Tri has become a compelling destination for travelers interested in history, nature, and local culture. Its reputation as a center for historical tourism is well established, with iconic landmarks such as the Quang Tri Ancient Citadel, Hien Luong Bridge, Ben Hai River, and Vinh Moc Tunnels leaving a lasting impression on visitors.

Beyond its historical importance, Quang Tri also boasts a diverse natural landscape. From serene beaches and towering mountains to peaceful villages and rich Central Vietnamese cuisine, the province offers a wide range of experiences. It is also a gateway to some of Vietnam’s most remarkable natural wonders, including the renowned Phong Nha, Ke Bang cave system, recognized by UNESCO as a World Natural Heritage Site.
